# About

I have always been fascinated by graphics. As a young boy, I went to sleep with [*Mathematical Models*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematical_Models_(Cundy_and_Rollett)) by Cundy and Rollett under my pillow — a book of exquisitely strange physical forms, nets, and solids that felt like a secret language the universe was speaking just to me.

[**Spirograph**](https://www.playmonster.com/brands/spirograph/) was one of my favourite toys. There is something almost miraculous about the way rigid gears and a simple pen conspire to produce curves of such organic elegance. I spent hours on the floor, changing wheels, shifting holes, filling pages.

As a young man, I took that obsession to its logical extreme: a Ph.D. from Cambridge University in the mathematics behind these shapes — the geometry of curves, surfaces, and the algebraic structures that generate them.

---

Then, one day, the question no one else seemed to be asking finally became impossible to ignore:

> *What does Spirograph look like in three dimensions?*

I made myself a cup of tea and got to work.

---

Now retired in Bangkok, Thailand — the Land of Smiles — I finally have the time, the tools, and the perspective to take what I learned and make it **pretty**.

Lucid Geometry is the answer to that question. It is the toy I wished I had as a child, the research I could not quite justify as a mathematician, and the retirement project that keeps my mind sharp and my evenings interesting.

I hope it brings you the same quiet delight it brings me.
